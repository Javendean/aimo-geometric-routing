# Sample Geometry Problems (IMO-level)

## Problem 1
In triangle $ABC$, let $I$ be the incenter and $\omega$ the circumcircle. Let $D$ be the second intersection of line $AI$ with $\omega$. Prove that $DB = DI = DC$.

**Topic signals:** incenter, circumcircle, angle bisector
**Strategy hints:** Angle chasing (inscribed angles), or coordinate geometry with circumradius

## Problem 2
Let $ABCD$ be a cyclic quadrilateral with $AB \cdot CD = BC \cdot DA$. Let $E$ be the intersection of diagonals $AC$ and $BD$. Prove that the perpendiculars from $E$ to the sides of $ABCD$ have equal product of opposite pairs.

**Topic signals:** cyclic quadrilateral, Ptolemy-like condition, perpendicular distances
**Strategy hints:** Ptolemy's theorem gives the constraint; use power of a point

## Problem 3
Given a triangle with sides $a = 13$, $b = 14$, $c = 15$, find the radius of the circle tangent to side $a$ and to the extensions of sides $b$ and $c$.

**Topic signals:** excircle, tangent lengths, numerical
**Strategy hints:** Heron's formula for area, then excircle radius $r_a = \Delta / (s - a)$. Answer is an integer.
